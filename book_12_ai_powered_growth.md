## Chapter 2: Understanding AI Marketing Technologies

### Machine Learning in Marketing

**Supervised Learning**: Algorithms trained on labeled historical data to make predictions. Examples include lead scoring, churn prediction, and customer lifetime value estimation.

**Unsupervised Learning**: Algorithms that find patterns without labeled outcomes. Used for customer segmentation, anomaly detection, and market basket analysis.

**Reinforcement Learning**: Algorithms that learn through trial and error, optimizing actions based on rewards. Powers real-time bidding, email send time optimization, and dynamic pricing.

**Deep Learning**: Neural networks with multiple layers that can process complex patterns. Enables image recognition, natural language processing, and predictive analytics.

### Natural Language Processing (NLP)

NLP enables computers to understand, interpret, and generate human language:

**Sentiment Analysis**: Determining emotional tone in customer communications, reviews, and social media.

**Topic Modeling**: Automatically identifying themes in large text collections.

**Text Generation**: Creating marketing copy, email subject lines, and social media posts.

**Named Entity Recognition**: Extracting key information like names, dates, and product mentions.

**Language Translation**: Converting content for global markets.

### Computer Vision

AI that interprets visual content:

**Image Recognition**: Identifying products, logos, and scenes in images.

**Visual Search**: Allowing customers to search using images rather than text.

**Content Moderation**: Automatically filtering inappropriate user-generated content.

**Aesthetic Scoring**: Predicting which images will perform best in marketing.

### Predictive Analytics

Using historical data to forecast future outcomes:

**Propensity Modeling**: Predicting likelihood of specific customer actions.

**Time Series Forecasting**: Predicting trends and seasonality in marketing metrics.

**Cohort Analysis**: Understanding how groups of customers behave over time.

**Attribution Modeling**: Determining which touchpoints contribute to conversions.

## Marketing Metrics Dictionary

**AOV (Average Order Value)**: Total revenue divided by number of orders
**CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost)**: Total sales and marketing cost divided by new customers
**Churn Rate**: Percentage of customers who stop using service
**CLV (Customer Lifetime Value)**: Predicted total revenue from customer relationship
**Conversion Rate**: Percentage who complete desired action
**CPA (Cost Per Acquisition)**: Cost to acquire one customer
**CPC (Cost Per Click)**: Cost for each ad click
**CPM (Cost Per Mille)**: Cost per thousand impressions
**CTR (Click-Through Rate)**: Percentage who click on link
**Engagement Rate**: Total engagements divided by impressions or reach
**LTV:CAC Ratio**: Customer lifetime value divided by acquisition cost
**MQL (Marketing Qualified Lead)**: Lead judged likely to become customer
**NPS (Net Promoter Score)**: Customer loyalty metric
**Open Rate**: Percentage who open email
**ROAS (Return on Ad Spend)**: Revenue divided by ad spend
**ROI (Return on Investment)**: Net profit divided by investment cost
**SQL (Sales Qualified Lead)**: Lead vetted by sales team
**Unsubscribe Rate**: Percentage who unsubscribe from emails
